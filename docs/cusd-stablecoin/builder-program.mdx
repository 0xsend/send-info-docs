# Builder Program

Send is backing projects that build on Canton Network. The CUSD Ecosystem Fund — capitalized by revenue from CUSD reserve yield — provides $CC grants to teams integrating CUSD into their products. If you're building on Canton, this fund exists to accelerate your work.

## What We Fund

Grants support projects that natively integrate CUSD in ways that expand the Canton ecosystem. Examples of fundable integrations:

- **Payments** — using CUSD as a settlement or checkout currency
- **Treasury & Custody** — holding or managing CUSD on behalf of users or institutions
- **Lending & Credit** — accepting CUSD as collateral or supply
- **DeFi Primitives** — AMMs, yield strategies, or structured products built around CUSD
- **Developer Tools & Infrastructure** — SDKs, APIs, and tooling that make CUSD easier to build with
- **Consumer Applications** — wallets, savings, or commerce apps that surface CUSD to end users

The deeper the CUSD integration, the stronger the proposal.

## What Makes a Strong Proposal

We prioritize applications that show:

- **Clear CUSD integration** — not a generic "add a stablecoin" plan
- **Technical readiness** — a working prototype, mainnet target, or detailed build plan
- **User or institutional demand** — evidence of real traction or pipeline
- **Canton-native thinking** — leveraging privacy, selective disclosure, or Canton's unique properties
- **Sustainable model** — not dependent on ongoing grants to survive

Grant size is matched to the scope and impact of the integration.

## How to Apply

Send a short proposal to **builders@send.app** including:

1. **Project name and team** — who you are, what you've shipped
2. **What you're building** — a brief overview and current status
3. **CUSD integration** — how CUSD fits into your product and what you need from us
4. **Milestones and timeline** — what you plan to ship and when
5. **Ask** — what support would accelerate your work (grant, liquidity, introductions, etc.)
6. **Links** — demo, repo, website, X, or prior work

No template required. Clarity beats formality.

## What Happens Next

1. **Review** — we'll respond within 5–7 business days to acknowledge and schedule a call if there's mutual fit
2. **Diligence** — technical and product review with our team
3. **Proposal** — if approved, we send grant terms including milestones, deliverables, and $CC allocation
4. **Execution** — grant distributed in tranches tied to milestones
5. **Ongoing** — we amplify your launch and connect you with the broader Canton ecosystem

## Hackathons & Community Events

We're actively interested in supporting Canton-focused hackathons, workshops, and meetups. If you're organizing something — or planning to bring CUSD into a broader Canton event — reach out. We can support with:

- $CC prize pools for CUSD integration tracks
- Technical mentorship from the Send team
- Co-marketing and promotion across Send channels
- Post-event follow-through for standout projects

Contact **builders@send.app** with event details.

## Partnerships Beyond Grants

Not every fit is a grant. We also work with teams on:

- **Pool Party liquidity** — deepening trading pairs that benefit your product
- **CUSD primary issuance** — institutional mint and redemption flows via Brale
- **Technical introductions** — to Canton Network, Brale, or other ecosystem partners
- **Joint launches** — coordinated go-to-market with overlapping audiences

If you're building something adjacent — infrastructure, research, education — we want to hear about it.

## The Fund

All grants are issued from the CUSD Ecosystem Fund, a dedicated multisig holding $CC acquired through the fund's operations. See the [CUSD Ecosystem Fund](./buybacks) page for transparency data — capital available, acquisitions to date, and allocation framework.

:::info Disclaimer
Grants are discretionary and subject to Send Foundation's review. Submission of a proposal does not guarantee funding. Grant terms, sizes, and recipients are at the fund's sole discretion and may change at any time. See the [Campaign Terms](/docs/legal/campaign-terms) for full terms.
:::
