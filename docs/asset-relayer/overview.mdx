## How It Works

Your asset on Base gets locked. A wrapped version is minted on Canton and lands in your Canton wallet. From there, you can hold it, transfer it to another Canton wallet, or use it as the foundation for trading pairs as liquidity pools come online.

## Security Model

Asset Relayer is built on the foundation of Circle's battle-tested **xReserve Contracts** and **Send Safe** (which uses Canton's decentralized party and DAML signature primitives).

| Property | Implementation |
|----------|---------------|
| **Reserve contracts** | Built on Circle's xReserve framework |
| **Signature model** | Canton DAML signature primitives via Send Safe |
| **Multisig quorum** | Majority `n of m` with at least three independent signers |
| **Signer eligibility** | All signers part of Canton Foundation or a supervalidator |
| **Onboarding** | Every signer must complete a rigorous opsec checklist and attestation before approval |

The design prevents single points of failure or collusion. No individual signer can authorize a bridge transfer; no validator can act unilaterally.

## What It Means for You

If you already hold SEND, USDC, cbBTC, or frxUSD on Base, those balances have a path into Send App on Canton through Asset Relayer. Bridged supply is the foundation for trading pairs and liquidity pools planned for later phases of the rollout.

:::info Disclaimer
Asset Relayer is bridge infrastructure built by Send Foundation. Wrapped tokens (the `.B` versions) are claims against locked reserves on Base — they are not the original tokens. Bridging involves smart contract risk, including but not limited to vulnerabilities in the reserve contracts, signing infrastructure, or underlying networks. Wrapped token names may change as the product matures. This page is informational and does not constitute investment or financial advice.
:::
